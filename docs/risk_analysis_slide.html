Risk Analysis — a Three-Phase Coopetition

Two opposing analysts cooperate on what to worry about, then compete on how risky it is — and their scores are carried forward un-averaged for the Leader to reconcile.
Phase 1 · Cooperate

Shared risk agenda

One joint pass merges 10-K narrative risks with the financial picture into a single, de-duplicated list of material risk factors.

→
Phase 2 · Compete

Two independent scores

Qualitative
10-K narrative → 0–10 risk
Quantitative
financials + price → 0–10 risk
→
Phase 3 · Keep both

Un-averaged pair

RiskAssessment = shared factors + both method-tagged scores. No blending — the Leader must reconcile the disagreement explicitly.

What data each side reads

Cooperate10-K (RAG) + structured financials — only to enumerate factors
Qualitative10-K only via search_10k: Item 1A risk factors, litigation, competition, regulation, cyber, supply chain
Quantitativefinancials (leverage · liquidity · coverage · margins · valuation) + pre-T0 price trend; no tools
Never usednews / social (→ Sentiment agent) · target price (the answer key)
